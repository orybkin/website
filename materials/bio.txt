Oleg is a Ph.D. student in the GRASP laboratory at the University of Pennsylvania advised by Kostas Daniilidis. He received his Bachelor's degree cum laude from Czech Technical University in Prague in 2017. He is interested in deep learning, computer vision, and learning in control. His recent work was on using deep predictive models to discover semantic structure in video and leverage it for planning and control. Prior to PhD, he worked on camera geometry as an undergraduate researcher advised by Tomas Pajdla at the Center for Machine Perception at CTU. He was a visiting student researcher at the Institut National de Recherche en Informatique et Automatique (INRIA) advised by Josef Sivic in 2016, Tokyo Institute of Technology advised by Akihiko Torii in 2017, and UC Berkeley advised by Sergey Levine and Chelsea Finn in 2019.
